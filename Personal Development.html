Twelve Pillars

_________________________________________________________________



Twelve Pillars by Jim Rohn and Chris Widener is truly an inspiring and I would definitely recommend it to anyone. This novel is about a man named Micheal, who met this caretaker of a nearby large property, Charlie, when his car shut down in a seemingly sparsely populated area. They become good friends over the next couple of months as Charlie teaches Micheal, unhappy with his life, the "12 pillars of success", before he passes away from terminal cancer. It entertainingly brings across how you could put your best foot forward effectively in order to achieve success.

Although I had already known some of these methods, due to my own trial and error, it was nice being reminded of them. Below are the ten parts in no particular order that stuck out to me the most whilst reading this novel. Hopefully it will be of significance with you as well.

  1. Writing down your goals
  2. Just decide what you want to do and then get started
  3. ‘What effect do my friends have on me,’ you’ve got to be willing to take an honest look at what kind of influence they currently have on you. In other words, are they helping you grow in the direction you want? What if keeping your associations the same, not making room for expanding any new associations or limiting your current relationships meant the difference in becoming what you want and achieving the life of your dreams for you and your family?”
  4. “Formal education will make you a living. Self-education will make you a fortune.” A successful person is always a life-long learner. Self-education is what you teach yourself. It is what you learn along the way so that you are constantly improving and growing. How will you ever grow and expand if you aren’t consistently and proactively pursuing knowledge and ideas that will move your life forward?”
  5. Time is shorter than any of us would imagine. If more people would wake up to that fact, more people would live differently
  6. ‘Work full-time on your job and part-time on your fortune.’
  7. Anything of significance is going to be hard
  8. ‘Communication is two or more people working together to find the common ground of understanding. And when they find that common ground, they are positioned to have tremendous power together.’...‘Communication isn’t just what you say. It is also how you say it, when you say it, and the receptiveness of who you say it to. And that’s just the half of it. The other half is making sure you really listen.’ It is the character behind the skill. First and foremost you have to care enough and value the other person enough to want to listen ...most people aren’t listening, they are merely waiting for the other person to take a breath so they can interrupt them and speak their own mind.”
  9. “Live a life that will help others spiritually, intellectually, physically, financially and relationally. Live a life that serves as an example what an exceptional life can look like.”
  10. ‘You cannot speak that which you do not know. You cannot that which you do not feel. You cannot translate that which you do not have. And you cannot give that which you do not possess. To give it and share it, and for it to be effective, you first need to have it.’

To me, the best way to apply things to your life, is by actually making an attempt to do it. All we need is a little step forward, it truly goes a long way towards our divine destinies. As the character Micheal did in the novel, I too would start out by trying to achieve one goal at a time, then build by adding on more as I go along, carving my own path to success. This novel did a splendid job of breaking down success to show its achieveability. It was truly inspiring.
